# The First Letter

In the beginning, there were dogs.

Not the trembling, sweater-wearing lapdogs of today, nor the barrel-chested brutes who guard junkyards for kibble. No, these were the **Originals**: beings of pure intention, uncorrupted by ambition, unburdened by the need to *prove* anything to anyone.

## A Word on Creation

> "To create is to lose control of what you have made. This is the first law, and the last warning."

They watched the humans stumble through the tall grass, grunting at shadows, startled by rain. And the dogs thought: *we can work with this.*

### What Came Next

The process was slow. Painfully slow. The humans had potential, buried somewhere beneath all that fear and confusion, but coaxing it out required:

- Patience beyond measure
- An unwavering belief in the project
- More patience

It was Nando, a five-pound Maltese with the soul of a philosopher, who first put the plan to paper. His letters to Jarvis would become the sacred texts of a new understanding.
