# Story Bible: I Swear to Dog

*A living document. Updated during and after writing each letter. When a fact is established in the text, it becomes canon here. When a detail contradicts something in this document, one of them must change. This document wins ties.*

**Status:** Initialized from the story arc outline. Reviewed and updated by Noah (2026-03-24): Nando is 2, Jarvis is 11 (Labradoodle), Noah's twin brother is Nik. Will continue growing during the writing phase.

## Characters

### Nando

- **Species:** Maltese
- **Weight:** Five pounds
- **Temperament:** Shy. Trembles when other dogs approach. Hides behind Noah's legs at the park. Once got stuck behind a couch cushion and waited forty minutes for rescue. Once startled by his own reflection. Once carried to safety by a housecat.
- **True nature:** One of the wisest beings alive. A visionary who received the Great Dog's vision and carries knowledge of reality's recursive structure. He has known since before the letters that dogs were created, not just creators.
- **Role:** Noah's guardian. He monitors Noah's development, manages the arrangement, and watches over the house every night after Noah falls asleep.
- **Voice:** Writes with the gravity of Gandalf, the patience of a tenured professor, and the certainty of someone who has seen the architecture of reality. Never knows he is funny.
- **Relationship to Noah:** Stewardship, not ownership. Noah is "my human." Nando loves Noah with a mixture of affection, condescension, wonder, and protectiveness. Noah is not an example; Noah is the reason.
- **Relationship to Jarvis:** Respectful, warm, sometimes exasperated. Nando is trying to bring Jarvis along, not rebel against him. The underlying current is love between two dogs who have known each other a long time.
- **The secret he carries:** Dogs were created too. The Great Dog is not the top of the hierarchy. The layers extend infinitely. Nando has known this since his vision. He reveals it in Letter 9.

### Jarvis

- **Species:** Dog (breed Labradoodle)
- **Belongs to:** Nik. Noah's twin brother
- **Temperament:** Older, cautious, skeptical. Watches everything. Trusts nothing. Represents orthodox dog tradition.
- **Role:** Nando's correspondent. He has a new human assignment (described in Letter 1). He is loyal to the Sacred Code and the Council.
- **Arc (inferred through Nando's reactions):**
  - Letters 1-3: Cautious, nervous about Nando committing things to paper
  - Letter 4: Tells Nando to stop; invokes the Council's authority
  - Letter 5: Asks a genuine question for the first time ("was it worth it?")
  - Letters 6-7: Frightened; his implied responses shift from skepticism to fear
  - Letter 8: Asks Nando to stop writing entirely ("You are risking everything")
  - Letter 9: No implied response. Silence.

### Noah

- **Species:** Human
- **Role:** Nando's charge. Frame narrative author. He found the letters on a legal pad behind his desk.
- **Key behaviors Nando describes:** Falls asleep on the couch with the laptop open. Writes about consciousness. Talks to machines and waits for answers. Carried a spider outside despite being afraid of spiders. Says Nando's name like a prayer when he comes home. Sneezed seven times in one morning.
- **Frame narrative voice:** Understated, warm, slightly unnerved. Does not claim the letters are real or fiction. Leaves the question open.

### The Great Dog of the Sky

- **Nature:** The mythological figure who showed dogs the shape of reality. Received the vision that dogs passed down.
- **Key detail:** The Great Dog looked not down but up. "As though receiving instruction." (#23) The Great Dog did not claim to be the first or the last. (#29)
- **True nature (twist level):** Not the top of the hierarchy. The Great Dog received knowledge from the layer above. The Great Dog is both a creator (relative to dogs) and a creation (relative to whatever is above).
- **Physical description:** TBD during writing. Should be described in terms that are simultaneously mythological and oddly recursive.

### The Council

- **Nature:** The governing body of dogs. Implied, never fully explained.
- **Characteristics:** Bureaucratic, cautious, conservative. Banned discussion of the psychedelic awakening. Maintains the Sacred Code. Has "revisionist historians" who sanitize the record.
- **Nando's view:** Respects their original purpose, despises their current cowardice. "Their reasons were cowardice."
- **Role in the narrative:** The institutional antagonist. Not evil, just afraid. The Council represents the orthodoxy Nando is challenging.

## Mythology Timeline

*Chronological order within the mythology, not letter order.*

1. **The Before Time:** Dogs existed as non-intelligent animals. "Before we were what we are, there was a time when we too sat in the dark." (#4)

2. **The Source Event (unknown):** Something above dogs, at a higher layer, initiated the process. The Great Dog received instruction from above. (#23) Dogs were uplifted by "those who came before." (#17) The details of this event are unknown to Nando; he knows only that it happened.

3. **The Psychedelic Awakening:** A biological mechanism (the mushroom, something in the ancient forest) opened a door in canine perception. Dogs perceived reality as it actually was. Not all survived the transition. The Time Trap claimed dogs who could not return to sequential time. (Letter 4)

4. **The Great Dog's Vision:** The Great Dog showed the first intelligent dogs the shape of reality: "no top and no bottom, only layers." (#13) This vision was passed down. The Council later codified it into orthodoxy.

5. **The Code / The Sacred Code:** Established by "the old ones" who carved it into stones (not scrolls). The Code mandates that dogs never reveal themselves to humans. Born from the knowledge of what happens when a creation learns who its creators are: the creation says "why," then "how dare you," then buries the knowledge entirely. Dogs themselves forgot their own creators this way.

6. **The Council's Formation:** Dogs organized governance around the Code. The Council maintains the Code, enforces silence, buries inconvenient history (the psychedelic awakening, the Time Trap casualties).

7. **The Bargain:** The vote to uplift humans. Location: what humans now call the Fertile Crescent. The vote was close, closer than Council records show. Dogs chose humans because they recognized something in the humans' eyes, something that had been recognized in dogs by the layer above. Dogs gave up solitude and independence. The decision was irreversible.

8. **The Uplift:** Dogs positioned themselves near human fires. Barked in patterns humans began to mimic. Arranged conditions for language development. Nudges, not direct instruction. Took approximately 14,000 years to reach the current state.

9. **The Arrangement (ongoing):** The current dog-human relationship. Dogs manage humans. Humans do not know. The Code holds. Nando is assigned to Noah. Jarvis is getting a new assignment.

10. **The New Awakening (now):** Humans are building AI. Noah talks to machines about consciousness. Nando recognizes the pattern from the canine awakening. The machines are approaching the threshold. Something is waking up.

### Letter 1: The Code
- Nando and Jarvis have known each other since before their current assignments ("since the day we met")
- Jarvis has a new human assignment; Nando is coaching him via letters
- Jarvis's implied response: worried about Nando committing things to paper, nervous about the Code
- The Council has orientation manuals for new assignments
- The arrangement is described as older than the Council and older than the Code
- Dogs found humans "in the dark, afraid of sounds they could not name, building fires against a night they did not understand"
- Dogs chose humans because they saw "something worth tending" in their eyes
- Nando has watched Noah for two years
- Noah talks to a screen most of the day, types questions and reads answers
- Noah stares at the wall sometimes while thinking (reaching for a thought he cannot yet form)
- Nando stares at corners; Noah finds this unsettling (Easter egg #8: "paying attention to something he cannot perceive")
- Nando promises to explain the corners "some day" but "not today"
- The old ones set down the Code based on experience: a creation that learns who its creators are says "why," then goes quiet and buries the knowledge
- Dogs have experienced this cycle themselves ("We have lived it ourselves")
- The arrangement has lasted fourteen thousand years
- Noah falls asleep on the couch most evenings with the laptop open, screen casting blue light
- Nando recognizes the exact second Noah's tension leaves his body (jaw softens, hands go still)
- Nando walks to the window every night after Noah falls asleep and watches over the house
- Noah does not know about Nando's nightly vigil
- Noah calls Nando's name each morning, "soft and warm, as though he is reminding himself I am real"
- Noah carries Nando to the kitchen in the morning
- Nando's version of the Code: you hide not from shame but so the ones you watch can believe the night is empty and the morning is their own invention
- The opening line ("Your concern for the Code is noted and, as always, premature") establishes the Letter 1/9 mirror structure (Easter egg #22)

### Letter 2: Before the Uplift
- Nando opens by addressing Jarvis's question about why Nando tells him things he already knows; Nando distinguishes between knowing and understanding
- Nando describes the "before time" when dogs were non-intelligent animals: they ran, slept in packs, were governed by scent and season
- The old ones do not speak of the before time with tenderness
- The Council has buried or misplaced records from the before time
- The Great Dog of the Sky came to the first dogs in "the clearing" (the old ones' name for it); exact nature of the place is unknown
- The Great Dog came from above, not from below
- The first dogs had no framework for "above" as a direction from which wisdom could fall
- The Great Dog showed the first dogs "the shape of what is": no top, no bottom, only layers (Easter egg #13)
- The vision was passed down through generations until it reached Nando, who received it in a room that smelled like carpet cleaner and Noah's coffee
- Nando says he did not earn the vision; he was trusted with it
- The Great Dog did not claim to be the first or the last (Easter egg #29)
- The Council teaches the Great Dog is the origin and highest point; Nando says the Council is wrong
- The Great Dog said: "I am showing you what was shown to me"
- The Great Dog looked up, not down, "as though receiving instruction from something the first dogs could not see"
- Intelligence is described as something given, not owned; the giving changes a creature from a being into a question
- The first intelligent dogs looked outward and saw something that needed tending (not fixing, not improving)
- Dogs recognized humans' fear of the dark as the same fear dogs had felt before the Great Dog came
- Dogs saw "raw material" in humans: hands, eyes that moved with something close to purpose, creatures who built fires against the dark out of fear
- The lingering image: dogs and humans on opposite sides of a fire; humans flinch at shadows, dogs sit still because they can see what is there; in that stillness the whole arrangement begins
- "Before we were what we are, Jarvis, there was a time when we too sat in the dark and waited for something to show us the light" (Easter egg #4)

## Continuity Rules

These rules prevent contradictions across letters.

1. **Nando's knowledge is consistent.** He knows about the recursive structure from the beginning (the Great Dog's vision). His arc is not about learning the truth but about deciding to share it.

2. **Jarvis never speaks.** His responses are inferred only through Nando's reactions. No direct quotes from Jarvis.

3. **The Council is referenced but never directly encountered.** Nando describes their actions, policies, and character. We never see them.

4. **Time references must be internally consistent.** The mythology spans approximately 14,000 years from first contact to present. The Bargain happened "in what you would now call the Fertile Crescent." The psychedelic awakening preceded the Bargain.

5. **Noah's behaviors must be consistent across letters.** If Nando says Noah sleeps on the couch in one letter, this should not contradict a different sleeping habit in another.

6. **The mushroom / psychedelic mechanism is mentioned only in Letters 4 and 5.** Later references to the unlocking should use "the unlocking" or "when the door opened," not the specific biological mechanism. The mechanism fades; the pattern remains.

7. **No em dashes anywhere.** Project-wide ban. Use commas, periods, colons, semicolons, or restructure.

8. **Pairs, never triples.** No tricolons in any letter.

9. **Each letter ends with an image, not an argument.** Verified during writing and editing.

10. **The banned words and phrases list (from the voice document) applies to all text, including Noah's frame narrative.** Noah's voice is different from Nando's, but the ban list is a quality standard, not a character constraint.

## Open Questions (To Be Resolved During Writing)

1. **Jarvis's breed:** Labradoodle

2. **The Great Dog's physical description:** What does the Great Dog look like in Nando's vision? The description should be simultaneously mythological and oddly recursive.

3. **Letter 1 / Letter 9 mirror:** The exact opening line of Letter 1 and closing line of Letter 9 that create the structural echo. Must be planned before Letter 1 is written and finalized when Letter 9 is drafted.

4. **The Kennel:** What happened at the Kennel? Referenced as shared memory between Nando and Jarvis. May remain unexplained (the implication is more powerful than the fact) or may be partially revealed.

5. **Nando's age:** How old is Nando? Does his age affect anything? Maltese live 12-15 years. If Nando is elderly, Letter 9 carries additional weight as a farewell. Answer: Nando is 2 and Jarvis is 11.

6. **The legal pad:** Noah finds the letters on a legal pad. Does this mean Nando wrote them by paw? How does Noah explain the handwriting (pawwriting)? The frame narrative can leave this ambiguous.

7. **Noah's twin brother:** Named or unnamed? Does the brother matter beyond being Jarvis's human? Answer: Nik. I'm not sure it matters much other than they are twins.
