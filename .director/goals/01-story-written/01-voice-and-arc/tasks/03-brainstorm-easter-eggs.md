# Task: Brainstorm easter eggs and "hidden in plain sight" clues

## What To Do

Run a dedicated brainstorm session to generate easter eggs and clues that will be woven throughout the 9 letters. The "hidden in plain sight" theme is central to the story — dogs have always been right there, humans just never noticed. Brainstorm clues that work on two levels: an innocent surface reading on first read, and a recontextualized meaning after the Letter 9 twist. Categories to explore: wordplay (GOD/DOG reversals, embedded meanings), behavioral clues (dog behaviors reframed as cosmic significance), simulation hypothesis breadcrumbs, and meta-references (things that reward the tech/AI-savvy audience). Each clue should be assigned to a specific letter for seeding during writing.

## Why It Matters

Easter eggs must be brainstormed before writing begins so they can be organically woven into the letters. Bolting them on after writing feels forced. The "hidden in plain sight" theme is what makes readers want to re-read immediately — it's a core part of the shareability.

## Size

**Estimate:** medium

Creative brainstorm session. Needs to generate enough material for 9 letters (minimum 2-3 clues per letter) plus assign them to specific letters.

## Done When

- [ ] Minimum 20 easter egg candidates generated across all categories
- [ ] Each clue has an innocent surface reading AND a twist-aware reinterpretation
- [ ] Clues assigned to specific letters (minimum 2-3 per letter)
- [ ] A few "big" clues identified for key moments (especially Letters 1, 5, and 8 as setup for Letter 9)
- [ ] Noah has reviewed and selected favorites
