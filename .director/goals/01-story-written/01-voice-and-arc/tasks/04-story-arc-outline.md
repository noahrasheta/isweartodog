# Task: Map the 9-letter story arc with beat-by-beat outline

## What To Do

Create a detailed beat-by-beat outline for all 9 letters plus the frame narrative. For each letter, define: its primary function in the arc (what it ADDS that previous letters didn't), the escalation mechanism (new revelation, tonal shift, stakes raise, or perspective shift), the emotional register the reader should feel, what Jarvis's implied response would be, where easter eggs are planted, and the final lingering image. Map the overall tension curve — the story cannot be flat wisdom across all 9 letters. Define exactly how the simulation hypothesis twist is seeded in Letters 1-8 so Letter 9 feels inevitable, not sudden. Include Noah's introduction (setting, tone, how he "found" the letters) and epilogue (the Nando reveal, emotional landing).

## Why It Matters

This outline is the architectural blueprint for the entire story. Without it, each letter risks being an isolated essay rather than a chapter in a building narrative. The outline ensures narrative tension escalates, the twist lands, and every letter earns its place.

## Size

**Estimate:** large

The most complex creative task in this step. Requires synthesizing the voice document, easter egg assignments, research findings, and the overall story vision into a coherent 9-letter arc with specific beats.

## Done When

- [ ] Each of the 9 letters has: function, escalation mechanism, emotional register, implied Jarvis response, easter egg placements, lingering image
- [ ] Overall tension curve mapped (not flat — clear rises, breathers, and the climactic Letter 9)
- [ ] Simulation hypothesis twist seeding plan specific (which clues in which letters, dual-readable language)
- [ ] Frame narrative outlined (introduction setup, epilogue payoff)
- [ ] Noah has reviewed and approved the arc
- [ ] Story bible template created (will grow during writing with established facts per letter)
