# Introduction

I swear to dog.

I need to say that first, because what you are about to read is going to sound insane, and I want you to know that I am aware of how it sounds. I found these letters on a yellow legal pad wedged behind my desk. The pad was crumpled at the corners and covered in what I assumed were bite marks. Nando chews on things. He chews on socks and on the corner of whatever book I leave within reach. I figured he had gotten to the legal pad too. Then I unfolded it and started reading.

I sat on the floor for forty minutes.

I am not going to tell you what the letters say. You will read them yourself. What I will tell you is that I have spent two weeks trying to figure out who wrote them. I checked the handwriting against everyone I know. I asked my brother Nik, who has his own dog, an older one named Jarvis. Nik had no idea what I was talking about. I asked my wife. I asked the kids. Nobody had been in my office. Nobody had access to that desk.

The only creature who was in that room every day, without exception, while I sat at my computer and worked, was Nando.

Nando is a five-pound Maltese. He is afraid of the park and afraid of plastic bags that move in the wind. He trembles when strangers reach down to pet him. He sleeps on my feet while I work, and as far as I knew, the most philosophically ambitious thing he had ever done was steal a sock from the laundry and carry it to his bed like a relic.

I am not claiming my dog wrote these letters. I am saying I cannot figure out who else did.

Here is what I found. Draw your own conclusions.
